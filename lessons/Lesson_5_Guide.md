# Lesson 5: Managing Inventory

## Overview
Inventory management helps you track product stock levels and avoid running out of items during service hours.

## What You Will Learn
- How to view current stock levels
- How to update or restock inventory
- How low-stock alerts work
- How to add and edit products

---

## 5.1 Opening Inventory
Go to **Inventory** in the sidebar menu. This opens `inventory.php`.

Only **Admins** have full access to edit inventory. Staff can view stock levels.

## 5.2 Viewing Stock Levels
- All products are listed with their **current stock quantity**.
- Items are color-coded:
  - 🟢 **Green** — Sufficient stock
  - 🟡 **Yellow** — Low stock warning
  - 🔴 **Red** — Out of stock

## 5.3 Restocking a Product
1. Find the product in the inventory list.
2. Click the **Edit** button beside it.
3. Update the **stock quantity**.
4. Click **Save** to confirm.

## 5.4 Low-Stock Alerts
- The system automatically flags items that fall below the **minimum stock threshold**.
- These appear as alerts on the **Dashboard** and in the Inventory page.
- Admins can set or change the threshold for each product.

## 5.5 Adding a New Product
1. Click **Add Product**.
2. Fill in the product details:
   - **Name**
   - **Category**
   - **Price**
   - **Stock Quantity**
   - **Unit** (e.g., bowl, cup, piece)
3. Upload a **product image** (optional).
4. Click **Save**.

## 5.6 Editing a Product
1. Click **Edit** beside the product you want to update.
2. Modify any fields (name, price, stock, etc.).
3. Click **Save** to apply changes.

## 5.7 Deactivating a Product
- Products can be marked as **inactive** instead of deleted.
- Inactive products no longer appear in the POS terminal but their data is kept for records.

---

## Summary
The Inventory module keeps your stock levels accurate and sends alerts when items are running low — so your team is always prepared.
